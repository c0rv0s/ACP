# AGC Narrative Audit

This audit keeps the product language pointed at a fresh crypto-native credit primitive, not a recycled token launch template.

## Core Narrative

AGC is not a staking token, a generic lending market, or a yield farm. It is a Solana-native credit machine for autonomous markets.

The strongest public framing:

```text
AGC is scarce credit inventory for machine economies.
xAGC owns the expansion layer.
The protocol prints only when the balance sheet earns it.
```

The market-facing promise is not guaranteed yield. It is exposure to future credit capacity. If AGC becomes useful working capital for agents, apps, borrowers, and automated markets, then the network needs more credit inventory. xAGC is the long-duration claim on that growth.

## What Feels Fresh

- The token is tied to balance-sheet expansion, not emissions.
- The protocol does not pretend every Solana swap flows through one frontend.
- The system treats credit creation as something that must be earned by reserves, collateral, liquidity, oracle health, and repayment quality.
- Governance is explicit multisig control with hard-coded limits, not slow token-voting theater.
- xAGC is a claim on the growth machine, not a generic staking APY wrapper.
- Underwriters provide first-loss capital for real credit risk instead of farming inflation.

## Language To Avoid

Avoid phrases that make AGC sound like every other DeFi launch:

- staking
- passive yield
- emissions
- generic lending protocol
- governance token
- operator dashboard
- policy chamber
- protocol functions
- users can later
- should / tries / hopes

Use language that makes the mechanism legible:

- credit inventory
- expansion layer
- balance-sheet proof
- credit capacity
- first-loss reserve
- earned expansion
- defense cash
- machine working capital
- own the credit machine

## Public Story Arc

1. **Big Idea**
   AGC is credit inventory for autonomous markets. Agents, apps, bots, and automated businesses need spendable working capital, not just static dollars.

2. **Problem**
   Crypto has volatile collateral, fully reserved stablecoins, and governance tokens. It does not have a native credit unit that can expand with useful economic activity.

3. **Upside**
   AGC holders are early to the credit capacity story. xAGC holders own the expansion layer. Underwriters earn spread by backing real credit risk.

4. **Defense**
   Expansion turns off when the balance sheet weakens. Reserves, collateral, oracle quality, debt caps, and pause controls keep the machine bounded.

## Product Voice

The external voice is confident but not promotional fluff. It speaks like the team that built the system explaining it to serious buyers:

- Say what the protocol does.
- Say what it refuses to do.
- Explain why the mechanism is different.
- Avoid sounding like an engineer narrating implementation details.
- Avoid pretending risk does not exist.

## Current Copy Pass

The website and docs now emphasize:

- AGC as credit inventory, not a generic token.
- xAGC as the expansion layer, not staking.
- credit facilities as balance-sheet-backed credit creation, not open-ended borrowing.
- reserves and collateral as proof that expansion is earned.
- governance as constrained authority, not decentralization theater.
